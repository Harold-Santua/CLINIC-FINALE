Explanation of the System Flow

The Clinic Appointment & Patient Record System is designed to help clinics manage patient appointments and store medical records efficiently. The system follows a structured process from user access to record storage. The process begins when the user accesses the system.

The user can be a patient, clinic staff, or an administrator. To use the system, the user must either log in to an existing account or register a new account if they are a first-time user. This ensures that only authorized users can access patient and appointment information.

After logging in, the user is directed to the system dashboard, where different functions are available depending on the user’s role. Patients can book appointments, view their scheduled appointments, and check their medical records. On the other hand, administrators or clinic staff can manage appointments, add new patient records, update patient information, and monitor clinic schedules.

If a patient chooses to book an appointment, the system will prompt them to select a doctor, choose an available date and time, and confirm the appointment details. Once confirmed, the system automatically saves the appointment information in the database so it can be accessed by both the patient and clinic staff.

After the appointment is scheduled, the clinic administrator or staff reviews the appointment request. They may approve the schedule, reschedule it if necessary, or cancel it depending on availability.

On the day of the appointment, the patient visits the clinic and the doctor conducts the consultation. After the consultation, the doctor or clinic staff updates the patient’s medical record in the system. This may include the diagnosis, treatment, prescription, and visit history.

All updated information is then stored securely in the system database, allowing the clinic to keep an organized digital record of each patient. These records can be accessed later for follow-up visits or future consultations.

Finally, once the tasks are completed, the user logs out of the system, ensuring the security of the clinic data.
